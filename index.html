Common questions

Quick answers, with links to the full guides.

How accurate are the calculators?

They give careful estimates from well-established methods and the dates you enter. They are not a substitute for medical advice, so your midwife or GP can confirm anything specific to you. More about how we build them.

Is my information private?

Yes. Everything runs in your browser, we do not store the dates you enter and there is no account to create. Read our privacy policy.

How do I know when I am ovulating?

Ovulation usually falls about 14 days before your next period, with signs like clearer, stretchier cervical mucus. Our ovulation calculator estimates it, and the fertile window guide explains the signs.

When should I take a pregnancy test?

From the first day of your missed period for the most reliable result. Testing earlier can give a false negative. More in early signs of pregnancy.

How is my due date worked out?

It is counted as 40 weeks from your last period and refined by your dating scan, so treat it as the middle of a range. Try the due date calculator or read how due dates are calculated.

Does it cost anything?

No. Every calculator and guide is free, with no sign-up. Some product links may earn us a small commission, which keeps the site free and never changes what we suggest.
